Thomson's gazelle

Back Next

The Thomson's gazelle is a small, graceful antelope found in East Africa. It's known for its slender body, white underbelly, and distinctive black markings on its sides and back. These gazelles are quick and agile, making them great at escaping predators. They typically live in open grasslands and are often seen in herds. Their main diet consists of grass, and they can reach speeds of up to 50 mph (80 km/h) to evade danger.
